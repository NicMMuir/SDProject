Research

As a tool, the Student Predictor, enables students and educators to identify the current trajectory of their academic performance.

The following section explores a further set of possible questions that the Student Predictor can be used to answer.

  1. Which subjects do students struggle most with?
      For the first research question we will be exploring our results by taking a number of factors into account. Firstly we need to normalize the data, since we have to factor the amount of people taking the course against the results of people who have already completed it. We can further expand on how to explore the data here by implementing a form of Bayesian Analysis. In particular, using the formula: P(A|B) = (P(B|A)P(A))/P(B), which gives us the probability of a student passing given a condition. For example, "What is the probability a student will pass given their MATHS 2 marks are average or high?" This will help us assess which subjects students will struggle with the most.

  2. Which features contribute the least to a student's success?
      To determine which features a student will struggle with the most, we intend to apply either Information Gain Ranking or Principal Component Analysis. We also intend to use this diagram provided by our client/lecturer to guide our process.

  3. Which is more useful to student success, first or second year?
      We intend to solve this by first proposing a hypothesis, either saying first year is more useful or second year is more useful. By means of confusion matrices we will then explore if our initial hypothesis was correct or not and in doing so derive a solution to the question of which year is more important.
